# 📘 Lesson 10 — Order Blocks

---

## 🎯 Objective

Learn what **Order Blocks** are, how to identify high-probability Order Blocks, and how to combine them with **Market Structure**, **Fair Value Gaps (FVGs)**, and **Premium & Discount** for precise institutional trade entries.

---

# 📖 What is an Order Block?

An **Order Block (OB)** is an area where **large institutional buy or sell orders** entered the market, causing a strong impulsive move.

It represents the **origin of a significant price movement** and often acts as a zone where institutions may return to execute remaining orders.

> 💡 **Think of an Order Block as the footprint left behind by Smart Money before a major move.**

---

# 🏦 Why Do Order Blocks Matter?

When institutions execute large orders, they rarely fill their entire position at once.

As a result, price often returns to these areas to:

- Fill remaining institutional orders.
    
- Mitigate previous positions.
    
- Continue the existing trend.
    

This is why Order Blocks frequently become important reaction zones.

---

# ✅ Characteristics of a Valid Order Block

A high-quality Order Block should meet **all** of the following conditions.

---

## 1️⃣ Fair Value Gap (FVG)

A valid Order Block **must create an imbalance**.

> ⚠️ **No Fair Value Gap = No Order Block.**

If there's no imbalance, the area is simply a Supply or Demand Zone.

---

## 2️⃣ Strong Displacement

The move away from the Order Block should be:

- Sharp
    
- Aggressive
    
- High momentum 
    

Ideally, the move should resemble a strong **V-shaped expansion**.

This confirms institutional participation.

---

## 3️⃣ Pivot Candle

The Order Block is usually the **last opposing candle** before the impulsive move.

Examples:

- Bullish Move → Last Bearish Candle
    
- Bearish Move → Last Bullish Candle
    

This pivot candle becomes the Order Block.

---

## 4️⃣ Break of Structure (BOS)

The strongest Order Blocks lead to a **Break of Structure**.

A move that changes or continues market structure carries more significance than a move that simply creates momentum.

---

# 📈 Bullish Order Block

A Bullish Order Block forms before a strong upward move.

### Identification Process

1. Identify the **last bearish candle** before the impulsive bullish move.
    
2. Confirm that price creates a strong displacement.
    
3. Verify that a **Bullish Fair Value Gap** is formed.
    
4. Confirm the move results in a **Break of Structure**.
    
5. Mark the **High and Low** of the pivot candle.
    

> 💡 This marked candle becomes the Bullish Order Block.

---

# 📉 Bearish Order Block

A Bearish Order Block forms before a strong downward move.

### Identification Process

1. Identify the **last bullish candle** before the impulsive bearish move.
    
2. Confirm strong displacement.
    
3. Verify that a **Bearish Fair Value Gap** is formed.
    
4. Confirm a **Break of Structure**.
    
5. Mark the **High and Low** of the pivot candle.
    

> 💡 This marked candle becomes the Bearish Order Block.

---

# ⚖️ Order Blocks vs. Supply & Demand

Although they are related, they are **not the same concept**.

|Feature|Supply & Demand|Order Block|
|---|---|---|
|Purpose|Identifies institutional buying/selling zones|Identifies the precise origin of institutional order flow|
|Drawing Method|Range Method (entire consolidation)|Pivot Method (single origin candle)|
|Fair Value Gap Required?|❌ No|✅ Yes|
|Precision|Moderate|High|

> 📌 **Every Order Block is a Supply/Demand concept, but not every Supply/Demand Zone qualifies as an Order Block.**

---

# 🔄 Fair Value Gaps & Order Blocks

These concepts work together.

A common sequence is:

```text
Order Block
      ↓
Creates Displacement
      ↓
Forms Fair Value Gap
      ↓
Price Retraces
      ↓
Mitigates FVG
      ↓
May Continue into Order Block
      ↓
Trend Resumes
```

### Possible Entry Approaches

**Approach 1**

Enter after confirmation inside the **Fair Value Gap**.

---

**Approach 2**

Wait for price to retrace deeper into the **Order Block** before entering.

Both approaches require confirmation.

---

# 📍 Premium & Discount Rules

Order Blocks should always be evaluated within the current swing range.

### 📈 Bullish Market

Focus on:

- Bullish Order Blocks
    
- Located in the **Discount Zone**
    

---

### 📉 Bearish Market

Focus on:

- Bearish Order Blocks
    
- Located in the **Premium Zone**
    

> ⚠️ Avoid buying from Premium or selling from Discount unless your strategy specifically supports counter-trend trading.

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Ignoring the Fair Value Gap

Without an imbalance, the area is **not** a valid Order Block.

---

### ❌ Ignoring Market Structure

An Order Block becomes much stronger when it leads to a **Break of Structure**.

---

### ❌ Trading Against the Trend

Example:

Selling a Bearish Order Block while the higher timeframe remains strongly bullish.

Always align with the dominant market structure.

---

### ❌ Entering Without Confirmation

Never place a trade simply because price touches an Order Block.

Wait for confirmation such as:

- Internal Structure Shift
    
- Liquidity Sweep
    
- Entry Model
    
- Strong Price Reaction
    

---

### ❌ Ignoring Premium & Discount

Location matters.

- Buy from Discount.
    
- Sell from Premium.
    

An Order Block in the wrong part of the range has a lower probability of success.

---

# 🔄 When an Order Block Becomes Invalid

If price moves aggressively through an Order Block without respecting it:

- Consider the Order Block mitigated or invalid.
    
- Shift your focus to the **next unmitigated Order Block**.
    

> 💡 Markets evolve. Your analysis should evolve with them.

---

# 💡 Pro Tips

- Prioritize Order Blocks that create both a **Break of Structure** and a **Fair Value Gap**.
    
- Strong displacement often indicates stronger institutional participation.
    
- Don't focus on whether the pivot candle is bullish or bearish—focus on the candle that **originated the displacement**.
    
- The best Order Blocks occur when they align with **Market Structure**, **Liquidity**, **Premium & Discount**, and **Fair Value Gaps**.
    

---

# ⭐ Key Takeaways

- An Order Block is the **origin of institutional buying or selling**.
    
- Every valid Order Block must create a **Fair Value Gap**.
    
- Strong displacement and a **Break of Structure** increase the quality of an Order Block.
    
- Use the **Pivot Method** to draw Order Blocks.
    
- The highest-probability setups occur when Order Blocks align with **Market Structure**, **Fair Value Gaps**, **Liquidity**, and **Premium & Discount**.
