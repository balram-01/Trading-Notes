# 📘 Lesson 9 — Fair Value Gaps (FVG) / Imbalance

---

## 🎯 Objective

Learn what **Fair Value Gaps (FVGs)** are, why they form, how to identify them correctly, and how to use them alongside **Market Structure**, **Supply & Demand**, and **Premium & Discount** for high-probability trade setups.

---

# 📖 What is a Fair Value Gap (FVG)?

A **Fair Value Gap (FVG)**, also known as an **Imbalance** or **Inefficiency**, is an area where price moved so aggressively that it **skipped over certain price levels**, leaving behind an inefficiently traded zone.

This represents **unfinished business** where institutions may later return to fill remaining orders.

> 💡 **Think of an FVG as a price vacuum that the market often revisits before continuing its trend.**

---

# ⚡ Why Does an FVG Form?

An FVG forms when one side of the market completely overwhelms the other.

### 📈 Bullish FVG

- Strong buying pressure.
    
- Price moves aggressively upward.
    
- An imbalance is left **below** the current price.
    

---

### 📉 Bearish FVG

- Strong selling pressure.
    
- Price moves aggressively downward.
    
- An imbalance is left **above** the current price.
    

---

# 🔍 How to Identify a Fair Value Gap

A valid FVG is formed using **three consecutive candlesticks**.

### Step 1️⃣

Identify a **strong displacement candle**.

Characteristics:

- Large candle body
    
- Strong momentum
    
- Clear directional move
    

---

### Step 2️⃣

Examine the three-candle sequence:

```text
Candle 1   ➜   Candle 2   ➜   Candle 3
 Normal     Displacement     Follow-up
```

---

### Step 3️⃣

Mark:

- The **High of Candle 1**
    
- The **Low of Candle 3**
    

If there is an empty space between these two levels, that area is the **Bullish Fair Value Gap**.

For a **Bearish Fair Value Gap**, mark:

- The **Low of Candle 1**
    
- The **High of Candle 3**
    

If a gap exists, it forms the **Bearish Fair Value Gap**.

> 📌 **No gap = No Fair Value Gap.**  
> Strong momentum alone does not create an FVG.

---

# 🔄 The Market Cycle

Price constantly alternates between two states:

```text
Imbalance
     ↓
Balance
     ↓
Imbalance
     ↓
Balance
```

### 📌 What This Means

- Strong moves create imbalance.
    
- Price often retraces to restore balance.
    
- Once balanced, the trend may continue.
    

This is why FVGs frequently act as retracement areas.

---

# 🎯 Using Fair Value Gaps in Trading

FVGs should **never** be traded on their own.

Always use them within the broader market context.

---

## 📈 Bullish Market

Look for:

- Bullish Market Structure
    
- Price pulling back into a **Bullish FVG**
    
- FVG located in the **Discount Zone**
    

This creates a higher-probability buying opportunity.

---

## 📉 Bearish Market

Look for:

- Bearish Market Structure
    
- Price pulling back into a **Bearish FVG**
    
- FVG located in the **Premium Zone**
    

This creates a higher-probability selling opportunity.

---

# 📍 How Price Can React to an FVG

Price does **not** always react the same way.

Possible reactions include:

- Touching the edge of the gap.
    
- Reacting near the **50% midpoint**.
    
- Filling the entire gap.
    
- Briefly moving beyond the gap to sweep liquidity before reversing.
    

> ⚠️ Never assume the exact reaction point.

Wait for confirmation before entering a trade.

---

# 🎯 Using FVGs as Targets

Fair Value Gaps are not only entry zones.

They can also serve as **profit targets**.

Price often moves toward **opposing Fair Value Gaps** to rebalance inefficient areas in the market.

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### ❌ Trading an FVG Without Context

An FVG alone is **not** a trading signal.

Always consider:

- Market Structure
    
- Trend
    
- Premium & Discount
    
- Supply & Demand
    
- Liquidity
    

---

### ❌ Entering Immediately

Do not buy or sell simply because price touches an FVG.

Wait for confirmation such as:

- Internal Structure Shift
    
- Liquidity Sweep
    
- Entry confirmation model
    

---

### ❌ Ignoring the Higher Timeframe

A Bullish FVG may fail if the higher timeframe trend is bearish.

Always align your trades with the dominant market direction.

---

### ❌ Incorrect Identification

Do **not** mark every strong candle as an FVG.

A valid Fair Value Gap exists **only if there is a visible gap between the surrounding candles.**

---

# 📋 Execution Checklist

Before entering an FVG trade, ask yourself:

- ✅ What is the current Market Structure?
    
- ✅ Is the higher timeframe bullish or bearish?
    
- ✅ Is the FVG located in Premium or Discount?
    
- ✅ Is there additional confluence (Supply/Demand, Liquidity, etc.)?
    
- ✅ Has price provided confirmation?
    

Only enter when all conditions align.

---

# 💡 Pro Tips

- Strong displacement candles often create stronger FVGs.
    
- FVGs are **magnets for price**, not guaranteed reversal zones.
    
- The best FVG setups occur when combined with Market Structure, Premium & Discount, and Supply & Demand.
    
- Think of an FVG as a **high-probability reaction area**, not a standalone strategy.
    

---

# ⭐ Key Takeaways

- A Fair Value Gap (FVG) is an imbalance created by aggressive buying or selling.
    
- Every valid FVG is identified using a **three-candle formation**.
    
- Price often revisits FVGs to rebalance before continuing its trend.
    
- Bullish FVGs work best in **Discount** during bullish trends.
    
- Bearish FVGs work best in **Premium** during bearish trends.
    
- Always combine FVGs with Market Structure, Supply & Demand, Liquidity, and confirmation for higher-probability trades.
