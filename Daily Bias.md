# 📘 Lesson 15 — Daily Bias

---

# 🎯 Objective

Learn how to create a **Daily Bias** by analyzing higher timeframe market structure, liquidity, premium/discount, and market narrative before looking for any trade.

> 💡 **A Daily Bias is not a prediction—it is a trading plan with a clear invalidation.**

---

# 📖 What is Daily Bias?

A **Daily Bias** is your expectation of **where price is most likely to move over the next 24 hours** and the reasons supporting that expectation.

A proper Daily Bias helps you:

- Filter low-quality setups.
    
- Determine whether to look for **Buys** or **Sells**.
    
- Know when to stay out of the market.
    
- Trade with confidence instead of emotion.
    

---

# ⚠️ Core Principle

> **If you don't have an invalidation, you don't have a bias—you have hope.**

Every Daily Bias must answer two questions:

1. **Why do I believe price will move here?**
    
2. **What would prove me wrong?**
    

If the market proves your idea wrong, simply reassess the chart.

No revenge trading.

No forcing trades.

---

# 📋 The 5-Step Daily Bias Checklist

---

# 1️⃣ Identify the Higher Timeframe Trend

Start with the **4-Hour (H4)** chart.

### Look for:

- Overall Market Structure
    
- Most Recent Break of Structure (BOS)
    
- Swing High
    
- Swing Low
    

These define your current trading range.

> 🎯 The Higher Timeframe provides the market's overall direction.

---

# 2️⃣ Apply Premium & Discount

Use the **Fibonacci Retracement Tool** on the H4 swing.

- Draw from **Swing Low → Swing High** (Bullish)
    
- Draw from **Swing High → Swing Low** (Bearish)
    

This divides the range into:

### 📈 Premium Zone (Above 50%)

- Price is relatively expensive.
    
- Prefer looking for **Shorts**.
    

---

### 📉 Discount Zone (Below 50%)

- Price is relatively cheap.
    
- Prefer looking for **Longs**.
    

---

### ⚖️ Equilibrium (Around 50%)

This is the middle of the range.

Characteristics:

- Indecision
    
- Choppy price action
    
- Fake breakouts
    
- Lower probability setups
    

> 💡 Avoid trading in the middle of the range whenever possible.

---

# 3️⃣ Mark Liquidity

Liquidity often acts as the market's next destination.

### Important Liquidity Levels

- Prior Day High (PDH)
    
- Prior Day Low (PDL)
    
- Equal Highs
    
- Equal Lows
    
- Swing Highs
    
- Swing Lows
    

Ask yourself:

> **Which side contains the most obvious liquidity?**

Price often moves toward these levels before making its next major move.

---

# 4️⃣ Build the Market Narrative

Now determine **what story the market is telling**.

Ask:

### Is price...

- Continuing the Higher Timeframe trend?
    
- Pulling back against the trend?
    
- Preparing for a Market Shift?
    

---

### Trend Continuation

Higher and Lower Timeframes agree.

Example:

- H4 Bullish
    
- H1 Bullish
    

Look only for **Longs**.

---

### Pullback

Lower Timeframe temporarily moves against the Higher Timeframe.

Example:

- H4 Bullish
    
- H1 Bearish
    

Wait for the pullback to finish before trading with the Higher Timeframe trend.

---

> 💡 **Trade with the internal order flow, not against both Higher and Lower Timeframes.**

---

# 5️⃣ Define Your Invalidation

Every trading idea must have a point where it is no longer valid.

Your invalidation could be:

- Break of Structure against your bias.
    
- Price closing beyond a key level.
    
- Failure of your Point of Interest.
    

⚠️ A wick alone is **not** enough.

Wait for a confirmed candlestick close.

If your invalidation is hit:

- Close the idea.
    
- Reassess the chart.
    
- Build a new Daily Bias.

# 📈 Trade Execution Rules

A Daily Bias **does not mean enter immediately.**

Wait for:

- Liquidity Sweep
    
- Market Structure Shift
    
- Entry Confirmation
    

Only then should you look for an entry.

---

# 🎯 Taking Profit

Choose **one clear target** before entering.

Common targets include:

- Prior Day High (PDH)
    
- Prior Day Low (PDL)
    
- Opposing Supply or Demand Zone
    
- Opposing Order Block
    
- Unfilled Fair Value Gap (FVG)
    
- Major Liquidity Pool
    

> 💡 One planned target is usually better than constantly moving your Take Profit.

---

# ❌ 5 Common Daily Bias Mistakes

## 1. Building Bias on the 5-Minute Chart

Lower timeframes contain a lot of market noise.

Always begin with the Higher Timeframe.

---

## 2. Trading Based on Feelings

Statements like:

- "I feel bullish."
    
- "I think it will go up."
    

are **not** trading plans.

A valid bias should be supported by objective market structure.

---

## 3. Trading in the Middle of the Range

The equilibrium zone often contains:

- Fake breakouts
    
- Choppy movement
    
- Unclear direction
    

The highest-probability trades usually occur near **Premium** or **Discount**.

---

## 4. Changing Bias Every Candle

Don't change your analysis because of every small move.

Your bias should only change when your **invalidation** is confirmed.

---

## 5. Not Trusting Your Process

Confidence is built through repetition.

The growth cycle is:

```text
Consistency

↓

Competence

↓

Confidence

↓

Conviction
```

Trust comes from collecting data—not from hoping.

# 📊 Daily Trading Routine

Use this routine before every trading session.

### Step 1

Open the **H4 Chart**.

---

### Step 2

Mark:

- Market Structure
    
- Swing High
    
- Swing Low
    

---

### Step 3

Apply Premium & Discount.

---

### Step 4

Mark:

- PDH
    
- PDL
    
- Equal Highs
    
- Equal Lows
    
- Major Liquidity
    

---

### Step 5

Write one sentence:

> **Today's bias is __________ because __________.**

Example:

> "Today's bias is bullish because price is in a Higher Timeframe discount zone, resting inside a demand zone, with Sell-Side Liquidity already swept."

---

### Step 6

Write your invalidation.

Example:

> "If H4 closes below the demand zone, my bullish bias becomes invalid."

---

### Step 7

Wait patiently for your Lower Timeframe confirmation before entering.

---

# 💡 Pro Tips

- Build your Daily Bias **before** the active trading session begins.
    
- Never force a trade just because you created a bias.
    
- A good Daily Bias tells you **when not to trade** as much as it tells you when to trade.
    
- Let Higher Timeframes determine direction and Lower Timeframes determine timing.
    
- Journal your Daily Bias every day—even on days when you don't take a trade.
    

---

# ⭐ Key Takeaways

- A Daily Bias is a structured trading plan, not a prediction.
    
- Always start with the **Higher Timeframe (H4)**.
    
- Use **Premium & Discount**, **Liquidity**, and **Market Structure** to build your narrative.
    
- Every bias must have a clear **invalidation**.
    
- Wait for Lower Timeframe confirmation before executing.
    
- Consistent execution and journaling build confidence over time.
    

---

# ✅ Daily Bias Checklist

Before placing any trade, ask yourself:

-  Have I identified the H4 market trend?
    
-  Have I marked the current Swing High and Swing Low?
    
-  Is price in Premium or Discount?
    
-  Have I marked PDH, PDL, and major liquidity pools?
    
-  Do I understand the current market narrative?
    
-  Have I clearly defined my invalidation?
    
-  Has the Lower Timeframe confirmed my idea?
    
-  Is my Take Profit based on a logical target?
    

> **Golden Rule:**  
> **Don't trade what you hope will happen. Trade what your Daily Bias and market structure objectively support.**
