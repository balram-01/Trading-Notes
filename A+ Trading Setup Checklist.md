# ⭐ A+ Trading Setup Checklist

> *"The best trades are the ones you almost don't have to think about—they simply satisfy your checklist."*

---

# 🎯 What is an A+ Setup?

An **A+ Setup** is a trade that satisfies **all five Golden Rules**.

If **even one rule is missing**, the trade is **not** an A+ setup.

> **Rule:** No checklist → No trade.

---

# ✅ The 5 Golden Rules

---

# 1️⃣ Bias Alignment

> **Trade with the market, not against it.**

## Definition

Your trade direction must align with the current market bias.

### Long Setup

- ✅ Higher Time Frame (HTF) is Bullish *(preferred)*
- ✅ Lower Time Frame (LTF) is Bullish
- ✅ Looking for Buy opportunities

---

### Short Setup

- ✅ Higher Time Frame (HTF) is Bearish *(preferred)*
- ✅ Lower Time Frame (LTF) is Bearish
- ✅ Looking for Sell opportunities

---

## Priority

```
Higher Time Frame
        ↓
Medium Time Frame
        ↓
Lower Time Frame
```

Always respect the higher time frame.

The lower time frame should simply provide your entry.

---

### ⚠️ Avoid

- HTF Bullish + LTF Bearish
- HTF Bearish + LTF Bullish

Contradicting trends create uncertainty.

**Beginners should stay out until both align.**

---

# 2️⃣ High-Probability Point of Interest (POI)

> *Great trades begin at great locations.*

A Point of Interest (POI) is an institutional area where price is likely to react.

---

## Valid POIs

- ✅ Demand Zone
- ✅ Supply Zone
- ✅ Order Block
- ✅ Flip Zone
- ✅ Liquidity Zone

---

## Checklist

- [ ] POI aligns with market trend
- [ ] Institutional footprint exists
- [ ] Liquidity is nearby
- [ ] Price has entered the POI

---

### ⚠️ Don't Trade

- Price approaching the zone
- Price between zones
- Price in the middle of nowhere

Wait for price to come to you.

---

# 3️⃣ Liquidity Sweep + Market Structure Shift

> **Confirmation before execution.**

Never enter simply because price touched your zone.

Wait for confirmation.

---

## Step 1 — Liquidity Sweep

Price should first remove liquidity.

Examples:

- Sweep previous highs
- Sweep previous lows
- Sweep Equal Highs
- Sweep Equal Lows

Purpose:

- Trigger retail stop losses
- Collect liquidity
- Allow institutions to enter

---

## Step 2 — Market Structure Shift

After the liquidity sweep...

Wait for structure to break.

Examples:

### Bullish Entry

- Liquidity swept below lows
- Break above last Lower High

---

### Bearish Entry

- Liquidity swept above highs
- Break below last Higher Low

---

## Entry Rule

✅ Liquidity Sweep

+

✅ Market Structure Shift

=

✅ Valid Confirmation

---

### ❌ No Confirmation?

No trade.

---

# 4️⃣ Timing (ICT Kill Zones)

> *Even the best setup needs the right timing.*

Institutional activity increases during specific trading windows.

These are called **Kill Zones**.

---

## Trade During Your Selected Session

Choose one session and master it.

Examples:

- 🌍 London Kill Zone
- 🇺🇸 New York Kill Zone

---

## Why Timing Matters

Inside Kill Zones:

- Higher volatility
- Better liquidity
- Cleaner price delivery
- Faster movement
- Better follow-through

Outside Kill Zones:

- Slow movement
- Random price action
- Fake-outs
- Choppy markets

---

### Rule

If your setup appears outside your trading window...

➡️ Skip it.

There will always be another opportunity.

---

# 5️⃣ Asymmetric Risk-to-Reward

> *Risk little. Aim for more.*

Every trade should offer significantly more reward than risk.

---

## Minimum Requirement

✅ **Risk : Reward ≥ 1 : 2**

Examples:

| Risk | Reward | Trade? |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 1R | 1R | ❌ No |
| 1R | 1.5R | ❌ No |
| 1R | 2R | ✅ Yes |
| 1R | 3R | ⭐ Excellent |
| 1R | 4R+ | 🔥 Exceptional |

---

## Stop Loss

Place it where your trade idea becomes invalid.

Never place it randomly.

---

## Take Profit

Target logical objectives:

- Opposing Supply/Demand Zone
- Liquidity Pool
- Swing High / Swing Low
- Fair Value Gap

---

# 🚫 When NOT to Trade

Sometimes the highest-quality trade...

...is **no trade**.

---

## Skip the Trade If:

- ❌ Bias isn't aligned
- ❌ Price is not inside a POI
- ❌ No liquidity sweep
- ❌ No market structure shift
- ❌ Outside your Kill Zone
- ❌ Risk-to-Reward is below 1:2
- ❌ No logical stop loss
- ❌ No clear target

> **If one rule fails, the setup fails.**

---

# 💡 The Principle of Inversion

Instead of asking:

> **"How do I make money?"**

Ask:

> **"What guarantees I lose money?"**

Avoid:

- Chasing price
- Trading randomly
- Emotional entries
- Ignoring structure
- Ignoring liquidity
- Trading without confirmation
- Overtrading

Avoiding bad trades is often more profitable than finding more trades.

---

# 📈 Position Sizing Rules

Never increase risk because:

- You lost money
- You feel confident
- You're emotional
- You "need" to recover

---

## Fixed Risk

Until you have a large sample of data:

- ✅ Risk **0.5%**
- or
- ✅ Risk **1%**

every single trade.

Consistency beats aggression.

Not activity.

> **The most profitable position in trading is often waiting.**
