# 📘 Lesson 18 — When **NOT** to Trade

---

# 🎯 Objective

Learn when to **stay out of the market** and avoid low-probability trades.

> 💡 **Professional traders don't make money by trading more—they make money by trading better.**

---

# 🧠 Core Philosophy

One of the biggest misconceptions in trading is:

> **"The more I trade, the more money I'll make."**

In reality:

- More trades ≠ More profits
    
- More trades = More opportunities to make mistakes
    

Most market movement is simply **noise**.

The best traders are patient enough to wait for **high-probability setups** that align with their trading plan.

> **Your edge comes from selective execution—not constant activity.**

---

# 📌 Golden Rule

Only trade when **all** of the following align:

- ✅ Higher Timeframe Bias
    
- ✅ Point of Interest (POI)
    
- ✅ Liquidity Sweep
    
- ✅ Market Structure Shift
    
- ✅ Entry Confirmation
    
- ✅ Acceptable Risk-to-Reward
    

If even **one condition** is missing...

> **Do not trade.**

---

# 🚫 1. Don't Trade in the "Middle of Nowhere"

## What Does It Mean?

Price is floating between key levels and is **not interacting with any Point of Interest (POI).**

Examples:

- No Supply Zone
    
- No Demand Zone
    
- No Order Block
    
- No Fair Value Gap
    
- No Liquidity Pool
    

---

## Why It's Dangerous

Entering in the middle of a range usually results in:

- Poor Risk-to-Reward
    
- Random price movement
    
- Emotional decisions
    
- Lower probability setups
    

---

## Correct Procedure

1. Perform your top-down analysis.
    
2. Identify your POIs.
    
3. Set price alerts.
    
4. Wait patiently.
    
5. Only look for entries once price reaches your zone.
    

> 🎯 **No POI = No Trade**

---

# 🚫 2. Avoid Slow & Choppy Markets

## Characteristics

- Sideways movement
    
- No clear trend
    
- Small candles
    
- Frequent fake breakouts
    
- Low momentum
    

---

## Why It's Dangerous

Choppy markets often:

- Sweep liquidity above highs
    
- Sweep liquidity below lows
    
- Reverse repeatedly
    

This creates uncertainty and increases the chances of getting stopped out.

---

## Best Action

Wait until the market:

- Breaks structure
    
- Shows momentum
    
- Develops a clear directional bias
    

> 💡 **A market without direction is a market without opportunity.**

---

# 🚫 3. Be Careful on Mondays & Fridays

## 📅 Monday

Why?

- Lower market participation
    
- Traders are returning after the weekend
    
- Liquidity is often inconsistent
    
- Higher chance of false moves
    

---

## 📅 Friday

Why?

- Institutions begin closing positions.
    
- Liquidity decreases.
    
- Weekend rollover approaches.
    
- Traders often take profits before markets close.
    

---

## Exception

If an **A+ setup** perfectly matches your trading plan, it can still be traded.

However, for:

- Day Traders
    
- Scalpers
    

it's generally better to stay out of the market.

---

# 🚫 4. Avoid Trading During December

## Why?

December is typically characterized by:

- Holiday season
    
- Institutional traders on vacation
    
- Lower trading volume
    
- Reduced volatility
    
- Slow and choppy markets
    

With less institutional participation, the market often lacks quality movement.

> 💡 Less liquidity usually means fewer high-quality opportunities.

---

# 🚫 5. Stay Out During High-Impact News

## Rule

Never trade immediately before or after major economic news.

Examples include:

- Interest Rate Decisions
    
- CPI
    
- NFP
    
- FOMC
    
- GDP
    

---

## The 30-Minute Rule

Avoid trading:

- 15 minutes before the news
    
- 15–30 minutes after the news
    

Wait for volatility to settle before looking for new opportunities.

---

## Why?

During major news releases:

- Volatility increases dramatically.
    
- Spreads widen.
    
- Stop hunts become more common.
    
- Technical analysis becomes less reliable.
    

There is **no statistical edge** during these periods.

---

# 🚫 6. Don't Trade in a Poor Mental State

## Trading is More Than Charts

The lesson emphasizes:

> **Trading is 80% psychology and 20% strategy.**

Even the best strategy fails if your mindset is compromised.

---

## Avoid Trading When You Are:

- Stressed
    
- Angry
    
- Distracted
    
- Emotionally overwhelmed
    
- Sleep deprived
    
- Mentally exhausted
    

Personal issues such as family problems, work stress, or relationship challenges can reduce decision-making quality.

---

## Why?

Emotional traders tend to:

- Force trades
    
- Ignore rules
    
- Increase risk
    
- Revenge trade
    
- Chase losses
    

> 🧠 **Protect your capital by protecting your mindset.**

---

# 🚫 7. Stop Trading During Losing Streaks

Losses are part of trading.

However, multiple consecutive losses require immediate attention.

---

## If You Experience 5–10 Consecutive Losses

### Step 1

Stop trading immediately.

---

### Step 2

Close your trading platform.

---

### Step 3

Take a break for at least one week.

---

### Step 4

Review your journal.

Ask:

- Did I follow my trading plan?
    
- Was my bias correct?
    
- Did I force trades?
    
- Was I revenge trading?
    
- Did I ignore confirmations?
    

---

### Step 5

Refine your plan.

Fix the mistakes before returning to live trading.

> 💡 **Break the losing cycle before it becomes a habit.**

---

# 🚫 8. Avoid the Last Trading Day of the Month

Many institutions close positions and rebalance portfolios at month-end.

This often results in:

- Reduced liquidity
    
- Irregular price movement
    
- Less reliable technical setups
    

Unless a high-quality setup appears, it's generally better to stay out.

---

# 🧠 The Power of Patience

Successful traders understand that:

> **Waiting is a position.**

Every trade you avoid that doesn't meet your rules is a victory.

The goal is not to trade every day.

The goal is to trade **only when the odds are clearly in your favor.**

---

# 🚦 Before Taking Any Trade, Ask Yourself

## 📈 Market Conditions

-  Is the Higher Timeframe trend clear?
    
-  Has price reached a valid POI?
    
-  Is the market trending instead of ranging?
    

---

## 📅 Timing

-  Is today a suitable trading day?
    
-  Is it outside major news events?
    
-  Am I trading during my planned session?
    

---

## 🧠 Mental State

-  Am I calm and focused?
    
-  Am I following my plan instead of my emotions?
    
-  Am I fully present?
    

---

## 📋 Trade Quality

-  Does this setup meet every rule in my trading plan?
    
-  Is the Risk-to-Reward acceptable?
    
-  Would I still take this trade if someone were watching every decision?
    

If any answer is **No**...

> **Do not trade.**

---

# ⭐ Key Takeaways

- More trades do **not** mean more profits.
    
- Never trade when price is in the middle of nowhere.
    
- Avoid slow, choppy, and directionless markets.
    
- Be cautious on Mondays, Fridays, and during December.
    
- Stay out before and after high-impact news.
    
- Never trade when emotionally compromised.
    
- Pause trading after a significant losing streak and review your data.
    
- Sometimes, the most profitable trade is **the one you never take.**
    

---

# 🏆 Golden Rule

> **Professional traders are paid for their patience—not for the number of trades they take.**
