# 📘 Lesson 8 — Premium & Discount

---

## 🎯 Objective

Learn how to identify **Premium** and **Discount** pricing using the swing range, and understand how institutions use these areas to improve trade entries.

---

# 📖 What is Premium & Discount?

The market can be divided into two pricing zones based on the **50% midpoint (Equilibrium)** of a swing range.

This helps determine whether price is **expensive** or **cheap** relative to the current trend.

---

## 💰 Premium Zone

The area **above the 50% Equilibrium**.

### Characteristics

- Price is relatively **expensive**.
    
- Better area for looking for **sell opportunities**.
    
- Institutions prefer selling here.
    

---

## 🛒 Discount Zone

The area **below the 50% Equilibrium**.

### Characteristics

- Price is relatively **cheap**.
    
- Better area for looking for **buy opportunities**.
    
- Institutions prefer buying here.
    

---

## ⚖️ Equilibrium (50%)

The **Equilibrium** is the midpoint of the swing range.

It separates:

```text
Premium (Expensive)
──────────────
50% Equilibrium
──────────────
Discount (Cheap)
```

> 💡 **Institutional Objective:**  
> **Buy at Discount. Sell at Premium.**

---

# 📐 How to Identify Premium & Discount

Follow these steps:

### 1️⃣ Identify Market Structure

Determine whether the market is:

- 📈 Bullish
    
- 📉 Bearish
    

---

### 2️⃣ Identify the Swing Range

Mark:

- Swing High
    
- Swing Low
    

These define the range you'll analyze.

---

### 3️⃣ Apply Fibonacci Retracement

Draw the Fibonacci tool across the swing range.

- Bullish Market → Swing Low ➜ Swing High
    
- Bearish Market → Swing High ➜ Swing Low
    

The **50% level** becomes your **Equilibrium**.

---

# 📈 Trading Rules for a Bullish Market

When market structure is **bullish**:

### ✅ Look for Buys in the Discount Zone

Your focus should be on:

- Demand Zones inside the Discount area.
    
- Pullbacks into Discount before continuation.
    

---

### ❌ Avoid Demand Zones in Premium

Demand Zones located in Premium are considered weaker and have a higher chance of failure.

---

# 📉 Trading Rules for a Bearish Market

When market structure is **bearish**:

### ✅ Look for Sells in the Premium Zone

Your focus should be on:

- Supply Zones inside the Premium area.
    
- Pullbacks into Premium before continuation.
    

---

### ❌ Avoid Supply Zones in Discount

Supply Zones located in Discount are considered weaker and have a higher chance of failure.

---

# 🎯 High-Quality Trading Zones

Not every Supply or Demand Zone is equal.

A high-quality zone should satisfy **two conditions**:

- ✅ Correct Supply/Demand Zone
    
- ✅ Located in the appropriate Premium or Discount area
    

> 💡 **Location increases probability.**

---

# ⭐ Extreme Zones

The closer a zone is to the **edge of the swing range**, the stronger it is considered.

### Bullish Market

The Demand Zone near the Swing Low has the highest probability of holding.

---

### Bearish Market

The Supply Zone near the Swing High has the highest probability of holding.

> 📌 **Rule:** The more extreme the location, the greater the probability of a successful reaction.

---

# 🔄 Trend Integrity

The trend remains valid only if the extreme institutional zone continues to hold.

---

## 📈 Bullish Trend

The **Extreme Demand Zone** should remain intact.

If it fails:

➡️ Market Structure may shift from Bullish to Bearish.

---

## 📉 Bearish Trend

The **Extreme Supply Zone** should remain intact.

If it fails:

➡️ Market Structure may shift from Bearish to Bullish.

---

# 🕒 Multi-Timeframe Considerations

Premium & Discount work on **every timeframe**.

However:

- Higher Timeframes define the overall market direction.
    
- Lower Timeframes are used for precise trade execution.
    

> ⚠️ **Don't mix unrelated timeframes.**

Example:

Using a **Daily Premium Zone** to execute a **5-minute trade** without additional confirmation can lead to poor decisions.

Always align multiple timeframes before entering a trade.

---

# 🧩 Confluence is Essential

Premium & Discount should **never** be used alone.

For high-probability trades, combine them with:

- ✅ Market Structure
    
- ✅ Supply & Demand Zones
    
- ✅ Liquidity
    
- ✅ Entry Confirmation
    

The more confluences present, the stronger the setup.

---

# 🔄 Counter-Trend Trading

Counter-trend trades are possible but carry additional risk.

Example:

- During a Bullish Trend, price enters the Premium Zone.
    
- A short trade may work temporarily.
    
- However, price can reverse at any time and continue the primary bullish trend once it reaches Discount.
    

> ⚠️ Counter-trend trades require extra caution and should never ignore the overall market structure.

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ Buying in Premium without a strong reason.

❌ Selling in Discount without confirmation.

❌ Ignoring Market Structure.

❌ Using Premium & Discount as a standalone strategy.

❌ Mixing higher and lower timeframes without alignment.

---

# 💡 Pro Tips

- Institutions aim to **buy cheap and sell expensive**.
    
- The best trades occur when **Market Structure**, **Supply/Demand**, **Liquidity**, and **Premium/Discount** all align.
    
- Zones located near the extremes of the range generally offer higher-probability opportunities.
    
- Think of Premium & Discount as a **price filter**, not an entry signal.
    

---

# ⭐ Key Takeaways

- **Premium** = Expensive price (Above 50%).
    
- **Discount** = Cheap price (Below 50%).
    
- The **50% Equilibrium** divides the swing range into Premium and Discount.
    
- In Bullish Markets, prioritize **Demand Zones in Discount**.
    
- In Bearish Markets, prioritize **Supply Zones in Premium**.
    
- Premium & Discount should always be combined with other market concepts for high-probability trading.
