# 📘 Lesson 2— Fundamentals of Price Movement

---

## 🎯 Objective

Understand **why price moves**, how markets transition between balance and imbalance, and how to objectively read price action.

---

# ⚖️ The Logic of Price Movement

Price **does not move randomly**. Every move is driven by the relationship between **buyers (demand)** and **sellers (supply)**.

### 📈 When Demand > Supply

- More buyers than sellers.
    
- Price expands **upward**.
    

### 📉 When Supply > Demand

- More sellers than buyers.
    
- Price expands **downward**.
    

### ⚖️ Fair Value (Equilibrium)

The market naturally seeks a state where **buyers and sellers are balanced**.

Think of price as constantly moving through this cycle:

```text
Imbalance → Balance → Imbalance → Balance → ...
```

> 💡 **Key Idea:** The market cannot remain in an imbalanced or balanced state forever—it continuously shifts between the two.

---

# 🔄 The Four Stages of Price

Every market cycle moves through these four stages:

|Stage|Description|
|---|---|
|🚀 **Expansion**|Strong directional movement caused by an imbalance between buyers and sellers.|
|↩️ **Pullback**|Price retraces toward fair value before deciding its next move.|
|⚖️ **Consolidation**|Buyers and sellers are balanced, resulting in sideways movement.|
|🔀 **Reversal / Continuation**|After consolidation, one side gains control, causing the trend to either continue or reverse.|

---

# 🏗️ Market Structure

Market Structure is the **skeleton of price**.

The market can only do **three things**:

### 📈 Uptrend

- Higher Highs (**HH**)
    
- Higher Lows (**HL**)
    
- ✅ Buyers are in control.
    

---

### 📉 Downtrend

- Lower Highs (**LH**)
    
- Lower Lows (**LL**)
    
- ✅ Sellers are in control.
    

---

### ➡️ Consolidation

- Price moves sideways.
    
- Buyers and sellers are balanced.
    
- High uncertainty and unpredictable movement.
    

> ⚠️ **Beginner Tip:** Avoid trading during consolidation until you can confidently identify breakout conditions.

---

# ⛽ The Role of Liquidity

Liquidity is the **fuel that allows price to continue moving**.

Think of liquidity like a **gas station**:

- Price pauses.
    
- Collects liquidity.
    
- Continues its move with fresh momentum.
    

### 📍 Common Liquidity Areas

- Above swing highs
    
- Below swing lows
    
- Around obvious support and resistance
    
- Near common chart patterns
    

---

# ❌ Common Trading Mistakes

### 1. Chasing Price (FOMO)

Entering during an **expansion** because you're afraid of missing the move.

**Result:** Poor entries, often just before a pullback or reversal.

---

### 2. Emotional Exits

Closing trades too early during normal pullbacks.

**Result:** Watching price continue in your original direction after you've exited.

---

### 3. Trading Assumptions

Entering reversal trades **before** the market confirms a shift in structure.

**Instead:** Wait for confirmation rather than predicting.

---

# 🎯 Goal of a Professional Trader

The objective is **not** to chase expansions.

Instead:

- Identify balance.
    
- Wait for confirmation.
    
- Position yourself before the next expansion begins.
    

> 💡 The best opportunities usually come **before** the big move—not after it.

---

# 👀 Reading a Blank Chart

Before placing a trade, ask yourself these five questions:

### ✅ Chart Checklist

-  Is price expanding, pulling back, or consolidating?
    
-  Who is currently in control—buyers or sellers?
    
-  Where are the obvious swing highs and swing lows?
    
-  Where is price most likely moving toward?
    
-  Is the market continuing its trend or preparing to reverse?
    

---

# ⭐ Key Takeaways

- Price moves because of **supply and demand**, not randomly.
    
- Markets continuously cycle between **imbalance** and **balance**.
    
- Every move follows four stages: **Expansion → Pullback → Consolidation → Reversal/Continuation**.
    
- Market structure is the foundation for understanding price.
    
- Liquidity acts as the fuel that powers market movement.
    
- Avoid emotional decisions such as chasing price or exiting too early.
    
- Always analyze a chart objectively before entering a trade.
