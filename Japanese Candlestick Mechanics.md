# 📘 Lesson 6 — Japanese Candlestick Mechanics

---

## 🎯 Objective

Learn how to read Japanese candlesticks, understand the battle between buyers and sellers, identify momentum, and use candlesticks **within market context** rather than relying on patterns alone.

---

# 🕯️ What is a Japanese Candlestick?

A **Japanese candlestick** is a visual representation of price movement over a specific time period.

Each candlestick shows:

- 🟢 **Open Price**
    
- 🔴 **Close Price**
    
- 📈 **Highest Price (High)**
    
- 📉 **Lowest Price (Low)**
    

> 💡 Think of every candlestick as a snapshot of the battle between buyers and sellers during that period.

---

## ⏳ Timeframe Matters

A candlestick can represent different periods of time, such as:

- 1 Minute
    
- 5 Minutes
    
- 15 Minutes
    
- 1 Hour
    
- 4 Hours
    
- Daily
    

> 📌 **Higher timeframe candles are formed by combining multiple lower timeframe candles.**

Example:

```text
1 Daily Candle
      =
24 × 1H Candles
```

---

# 🏗️ Anatomy of a Candlestick

A candlestick consists of **two main parts**:

## 📦 Body

The **body** represents the distance between the **Open** and **Close** prices.

### 🟢 Bullish Candle

- Close > Open
    
- Buyers were stronger during that period.
    

### 🔴 Bearish Candle

- Close < Open
    
- Sellers were stronger during that period.
    

> ⚠️ **Important:** A bullish candle does **not** automatically mean "Buy," and a bearish candle does **not** automatically mean "Sell." It only shows who controlled that specific candle.

---

## 📍 Wicks (Shadows)

The thin lines above and below the body are called **wicks**.

They show where price attempted to move but **failed to stay**.

### ⬆️ Long Upper Wick

- Buyers pushed price higher.
    
- Sellers rejected those higher prices.
    
- Indicates selling pressure.
    

---

### ⬇️ Long Lower Wick

- Sellers pushed price lower.
    
- Buyers rejected those lower prices.
    
- Indicates buying pressure.
    

---

# ⚡ Reading Momentum & Pressure

The size of the candle body tells you how much conviction is behind the move.

## 🚀 Large Body

Indicates:

- Strong momentum
    
- Strong conviction
    
- One side is clearly in control
    

---

## ⚖️ Small Body

Indicates:

- Weak momentum
    
- Indecision
    
- Buyers and sellers are nearly balanced
    

---

# 💪 BBC (Big Bullish / Bearish Candlestick)

A **BBC** is a candlestick with a large body showing strong momentum.

### Characteristics

- Large body
    
- Small wicks
    
- Strong buying or selling pressure
    

These candles often suggest that momentum may continue in the same direction.

---

# 📏 Body vs. Wick Analysis

The relationship between the body and the wicks provides valuable information.

|Candle Appearance|Interpretation|
|---|---|
|✅ Large Body + Small Wicks|Strong momentum and conviction|
|⚖️ Small Body + Large Wicks|High volatility, rejection, and uncertainty|

> 💡 **Rule of Thumb:** The larger the body compared to the wicks, the stronger the momentum.

---

# 🧩 Candlestick Patterns

The instructor advises **not** to rely solely on memorizing candlestick patterns.

Examples include:

- Engulfing
    
- Morning Star
    
- Evening Star
    
- Doji
    

> ⚠️ **A candlestick pattern is only meaningful when combined with market context.**

Context includes:

- Market Structure
    
- Trend Direction
    
- Support & Resistance
    
- Supply & Demand
    
- Liquidity
    

Without context, patterns have little value.

---

# ⚖️ Understanding the Doji

A **Doji** represents **indecision**.

Characteristics:

- Buyers and sellers are balanced.
    
- Neither side has gained control.
    

> ⚠️ A Doji **does not automatically signal a reversal.**

Always wait for confirmation before making a trading decision.

---

# 🔍 Candlestick Analysis Process

Before acting on any candlestick or pattern, follow this process:

### Step 1️⃣

Determine the **overall market trend**.

- Uptrend?
    
- Downtrend?
    
- Sideways?
    

---

### Step 2️⃣

Observe recent candle behavior.

Ask yourself:

- Is momentum increasing?
    
- Is momentum weakening?
    

---

### Step 3️⃣

Analyze the context.

Where is the pattern forming?

- At Support?
    
- At Resistance?
    
- Inside a Supply/Demand Zone?
    
- Near Liquidity?
    

---

### Step 4️⃣

Wait for confirmation.

Never trade solely because of a single candlestick.

Look for follow-up price action that confirms the intended direction.

---

# 🏆 Golden Rules

## 👑 1. Context is King

Candlesticks should **never** be analyzed in isolation.

Always combine them with:

- Market Structure
    
- Support & Resistance
    
- Supply & Demand
    
- Liquidity
    
- Overall Trend
    

---

## ⏳ 2. Higher Timeframes Carry More Weight

Higher timeframe candlesticks provide more reliable information than lower timeframe candles.

Example:

- 4H candle > 15M candle
    
- Daily candle > 1H candle
    

---

## 📈 3. Trade With Momentum

Use candlesticks to understand the current **heartbeat of the market**.

Trade **with** the existing momentum instead of fighting against it.

---

# ⭐ Key Takeaways

- Candlesticks reveal the battle between buyers and sellers.
    
- Every candlestick contains four key prices: **Open, High, Low, and Close (OHLC)**.
    
- Large bodies indicate strong momentum; small bodies indicate indecision.
    
- Wicks represent rejection of higher or lower prices.
    
- Candlestick patterns are only useful when supported by market context.
    
- Higher timeframe candles are generally more reliable.
    
- Always seek confirmation before entering a trade.
