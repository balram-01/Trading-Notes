---

## 🎯 Objective

Learn what **ICT Killzones** are, why they matter, and how to use them to trade only during the highest-probability periods of the trading day.

---

# 📖 What are ICT Killzones?

**Killzones** are specific periods of the trading day when the market experiences a significant increase in:

- 📈 Trading Volume
    
- ⚡ Volatility
    
- 💰 Institutional Participation
    

These periods often produce:

- Liquidity Sweeps
    
- Strong Breakouts
    
- Reversals
    
- Trend Continuations
    

> 💡 **The goal is not to trade all day—it is to trade during the best hours of the day.**

---

# 🧠 Why Trade During Killzones?

During Killzones:

- Liquidity enters the market.
    
- Institutions become active.
    
- Price moves more efficiently.
    
- High-quality trading setups appear more frequently.
    

Outside these periods, markets are often slower and less predictable.

---

|Killzone|IST Time|Market Characteristics|Best Currency Pairs|
|---|---|---|---|
|🌏 Asia Killzone|**5:30 AM – 9:30 AM**|Lower volatility, range-bound movement|AUD, NZD, JPY|
|🇬🇧 London Killzone|**11:30 AM – 2:30 PM**|High liquidity, strong trends, clean expansions|EUR, GBP|
|🇺🇸 New York Killzone|**4:30 PM – 7:30 PM**|High volatility, major economic news, strong momentum|USD Pairs|
|🌇 London Close|**7:30 PM – 9:30 PM**|Reduced volume, retracements and smaller opportunities|Major Forex Pairs|

> 💡 **For most Forex traders, the London and New York Killzones provide the highest-quality trading opportunities.**

---

# 🌏 Asia Sweep Strategy

One of the most common institutional behaviors is the **Asia Session Sweep**.

Institutions often use the liquidity built during the Asian session before moving price in the opposite direction.

---

## 📈 Bullish Sweep

Price:

1. Sweeps the **Asia Low**
    
2. Traps sellers
    
3. Reverses upward
    
4. Begins the main bullish move
    

---

## 📉 Bearish Sweep

Price:

1. Sweeps the **Asia High**
    
2. Traps buyers
    
3. Reverses downward
    
4. Begins the main bearish move
    

> 💡 The liquidity above the Asia High and below the Asia Low often acts as fuel for the next major move.

# 🎯 Choosing the Right Currency Pair

Different sessions favor different currencies.

|Session|Best Pairs|
|---|---|
|🌏 Asia|AUDUSD, NZDUSD, USDJPY, AUDJPY, NZDJPY|
|🇬🇧 London|EURUSD, GBPUSD, EURGBP, GBPJPY|
|🇺🇸 New York|EURUSD, GBPUSD, USDJPY, USDCAD, XAUUSD (Gold)|

> ⚠️ Don't expect EURUSD to produce the same movement during the Asia Session as it does during the London Session.

# 🛠️ The ICT Trading Workflow

## Step 1️⃣ Choose One Killzone

Select a session that fits your daily schedule.

Examples:

- Office worker → New York Killzone
    
- Full-time trader → London Killzone
    

Stay consistent.

---

## Step 2️⃣ Select Your Trading Pairs

Choose pairs that are most active during your selected session.

---

## Step 3️⃣ Build Your Market Bias

Before the Killzone begins:

- Identify Market Structure
    
- Mark Supply & Demand
    
- Identify Order Blocks
    
- Mark Fair Value Gaps
    
- Draw Liquidity Levels
    
- Identify Premium & Discount
    

Your preparation should already be complete before the session opens.

---

## Step 4️⃣ Wait Patiently

Allow price to reach your predefined Point of Interest (POI).

Do **nothing** until this happens.

---

## Step 5️⃣ Execute

Once price reaches your POI:

Look for:

- Market Structure Shift
    
- Liquidity Sweep
    
- Entry Confirmation
    

If all conditions align:

✅ Execute the trade.

If not:

🚫 Stay out of the market.

# 🚫 Avoid Overtrading

Many traders lose money because they believe they must trade all day.

In reality:

- More trades ≠ More profits.
    
- The best traders often take only one or two high-quality trades per day.
    

### Consequences of Overtrading

- Emotional decisions
    
- Revenge trading
    
- Analysis paralysis
    
- Giving back earlier profits
    
- Mental fatigue
    

> 💡 Sometimes, the best trade is **no trade**.

# ⚠️ Trading Rules

### ✅ Observe, Don't Predict

Never try to force the market to do what you expect.

Instead:

Observe price.

React to confirmation.

---

### ✅ Let Price Come to You

Don't chase candles.

Allow price to reach your predefined zones.

---

### ✅ One Session is Enough

Choose one Killzone.

Master it.

Avoid switching between multiple sessions every day.

- Prepare your charts **before** the Killzone starts.
    
- Trade only when all your confluences align.

- Missing a trade is better than forcing a poor-quality setup.
    
- Consistency comes from following a repeatable routine—not from trading more often.
