# 📘 Lesson 17 — Professional Trading Routine & Mechanical Trading Plan

---

# 🎯 Objective

Develop a **professional daily routine** and a **mechanical trading system** that removes emotions, improves consistency, and helps you execute trades like a professional.

> 💡 **The quality of your trading is determined long before you place your first trade.**

---

# 🌅 Pre-Market Routine

> Complete this routine **approximately 1 hour before your trading session**.

The goal is to prepare both **your charts and your mindset** before the market opens.

---

# ✅ Step 1 — Check the Economic Calendar

Before opening any charts:

- Check today's **high-impact economic news**.
    
- Filter events related to your watchlist.
    
- Mark important announcements.
    

### 🚫 News Trading Rule

Avoid opening trades:

- **15 minutes before** high-impact news.
    
- **15 minutes after** high-impact news.
    

> 💡 High-impact news can create unpredictable volatility and invalidate technical setups.

---

# ✅ Step 2 — Review Your Trading Plan

Refresh yourself on your trading rules.

Review:

- Charting process
    
- Entry criteria
    
- Trade management rules
    
- Exit strategy
    
- Risk management rules
    

> 📌 Never rely on memory—read your trading plan every day.

---

# ✅ Step 3 — Analyze the Charts

Follow your complete top-down analysis.

### Mark the Higher Timeframe Structure

Identify:

- Swing High
    
- Swing Low
    
- Break of Structure
    
- Market Trend
    

---

### Determine Daily Bias

Ask yourself:

- 🟢 Is today's bias bullish?
    
- 🔴 Is today's bias bearish?
    

---

### Mark Key Areas

Identify:

- Supply Zones
    
- Demand Zones
    
- Order Blocks
    
- Fair Value Gaps
    
- Liquidity Pools
    

These become your **Points of Interest (POIs).**

---

### Set Price Alerts

Once your zones are marked:

- Define your ideal entry locations.
    
- Set TradingView alerts.
    
- Wait for price to come to you.
    

> 💡 **Professionals wait for opportunities. Beginners chase them.**

---

# ✅ Step 4 — Review Existing Positions

If holding overnight trades:

Check:

- Stop Loss
    
- Take Profit
    
- Overall Risk
    

Only make adjustments **if your trading plan allows it.**

Never modify trades because of fear or hope.

---

# ✅ Step 5 — Prepare Your Mind

Spend **10–15 minutes** preparing mentally.

Examples:

- Meditation
    
- Deep breathing
    
- Visualization
    
- Quiet reflection
    

Purpose:

- Reduce stress
    
- Improve focus
    
- Shift from analysis mode to execution mode
    

> 🧠 A calm mind leads to disciplined execution.

---

# ⚙️ Simplified Mechanical Trading Plan

---

# 📊 Step 1 — Charting & Market Bias

## Determine the Trend

Identify Market Structure.

### 📈 Bullish

- Higher Highs (HH)
    
- Higher Lows (HL)
    

➡️ Look for buying opportunities.

---

### 📉 Bearish

- Lower Highs (LH)
    
- Lower Lows (LL)
    

➡️ Look for selling opportunities.

---

## Define the Swing Range

Locate the latest Break of Structure.

Mark:

- Swing High
    
- Swing Low
    

This becomes your working range.

---

## Apply Premium & Discount

Use Fibonacci settings:

```text
0

0.5

1
```

Draw:

- Swing Low → Swing High (Bullish)
    
- Swing High → Swing Low (Bearish)
    

### Premium Zone

Above 50%

➡️ Look for Shorts.

### Discount Zone

Below 50%

➡️ Look for Longs.

---

# 🎯 Step 2 — Define Points of Interest (POIs)

Within the Premium or Discount areas,

identify institutional zones such as:

- Supply Zones
    
- Demand Zones
    
- Order Blocks
    
- Flip Zones
    
- Liquidity Zones
    

### Rule

The closer a zone is to the extreme of the range,

the stronger the probability of a reaction.

---

### Patience Rule

Do **not** switch to a lower timeframe until price reaches your POI.

> **No touch = No trade.**

---

# 🎯 Step 3 — Entry Criteria

Once price reaches a POI,

move to your execution timeframe

(typically **15-Minute or 1-Hour**).

Look for confirmation.

---

## ✅ Market Structure Shift

Wait for internal structure to change.

Example:

Bullish internal structure breaks its last Higher Low

➡️ Bearish Shift

or

Bearish internal structure breaks its last Lower High

➡️ Bullish Shift

---

## ✅ Liquidity Sweep

Wait for price to hunt liquidity first.

Examples:

- Swing High sweep
    
- Swing Low sweep
    
- Equal Highs
    
- Equal Lows
    

Once liquidity is collected,

look for your entry confirmation.

---

# 🚀 Step 4 — Execution Models

## 🔥 Aggressive Entry

Enter immediately after:

- Liquidity Sweep
    
- Market Structure Shift
    

### Advantages

- Better Risk-to-Reward
    
- Earlier Entry
    

### Disadvantages

- Lower confirmation
    
- Higher risk
    

---

## 🛡️ Conservative Entry

Wait for price to retrace into:

- Supply Zone
    
- Demand Zone
    
- Order Block
    

created after the Market Shift.

### Advantages

- Higher confirmation
    
- Safer entry
    

### Disadvantages

- Possibility of missing the trade
    

---

# 🎯 Step 5 — Exit Strategy

## Stop Loss

Place your Stop Loss:

- Above the Supply Zone (Shorts)
    
- Below the Demand Zone (Longs)
    

The Stop Loss should invalidate the trade idea.

---

## Take Profit

Target logical objectives such as:

- Opposing Supply/Demand Zone
    
- Liquidity Pool
    
- Swing High
    
- Swing Low
    

> 🎯 Take profits at realistic levels instead of expecting every trend to continue indefinitely.

---

## Trade Management

This strategy follows a

> **Set & Forget** approach.

Once the trade is placed:

- No moving Stop Loss
    
- No partial profit taking
    
- No emotional adjustments
    

Allow the trade to play out according to the original plan.

---

# 🌙 Post-Market Routine

Your trading day doesn't end after closing the charts.

This is where long-term improvement happens.

---

# 📒 Trading Journal

Record every trade.

Include:

- Entry Screenshot
    
- Exit Screenshot
    
- Trade Idea
    
- Market Bias
    
- Emotions
    
- Mistakes
    
- Lessons Learned
    

Also ask yourself:

- Did I follow my plan?
    
- What could I improve?
    

---

# 📊 Performance Review

Track important statistics.

Examples:

- Win Rate
    
- Average R-Multiple
    
- Profit Factor
    
- Edge Score
    
- Rule Compliance
    

The goal is to improve your **process**, not just your profits.

---

# 🚧 Guardrail Review

Identify every rule violation.

Examples:

- Trading outside your session
    
- Ignoring Higher Timeframe Bias
    
- Overtrading
    
- Revenge Trading
    
- Breaking risk rules
    

Fixing these mistakes improves consistency more than finding a new strategy.

---

# 🔄 Reflection

Conduct regular reviews.

### Weekly

Review mistakes and improvements.

### Monthly

Measure progress and identify recurring patterns.

### Quarterly

Evaluate your trading system.

### Yearly

Assess overall growth and refine your trading plan.

Treat trading like running a business.

---

# 🧘 Mental Reset

End every trading day by clearing your mind.

Suggestions:

- Deep breathing
    
- Meditation
    
- Short walk
    
- Time away from charts
    

Never carry today's emotions into tomorrow's session.

Every trading day is a fresh start.

---

# 💡 Key Principles

## ⏳ Waiting Is a Position

The best trade is often **no trade**.

Patience protects capital.

---

## ⚙️ Trust the Process

Focus on executing your trading plan—not predicting the market.

Consistent execution produces consistent results.

---

## 📋 Clarity Creates Discipline

The clearer your system,

the easier it becomes to follow.

Confusion leads to emotional trading.

---

## 🤖 Mechanical > Emotional

A mechanical trader follows:

- Rules
    
- Data
    
- Statistics
    
- Backtesting
    

Not:

- Fear
    
- Hope
    
- Gut feelings
    
- Excitement

## 🌅 Before the Market

-  Check Economic Calendar
    
-  Review Trading Plan
    
-  Mark Higher Timeframe Structure
    
-  Determine Daily Bias
    
-  Apply Premium & Discount
    
-  Mark POIs
    
-  Set Price Alerts
    
-  Review Existing Trades

-  Higher Timeframe Bias confirmed
    
-  Price reached POI
    
-  Liquidity swept
    
-  Market Structure Shift confirmed
    
-  Entry model valid
    
-  Risk-to-Reward acceptable
    
-  Stop Loss defined
    
-  Take Profit defined
    

---

## 🌙 After the Market

-  Journal every trade
    
-  Record emotions
    
-  Review performance metrics
    
-  Identify rule violations
    
-  Reflect on lessons learned
    
-  Complete mental reset
    

---

# 🏆 Final Takeaways

- Great trading starts with great preparation.
    
- Never trade without a written plan.
    
- Let price come to your zones—never chase the market.
    
- Measure success by **how consistently you follow your system**, not by individual trade outcomes.
    
- Discipline, patience, and process will always outperform emotion.
    

> **Golden Rule:**  
> **A professional trader prepares before the market opens, executes only when every rule is satisfied, and improves after the market closes.**
