# 📘 Lesson 17 — Pre-Market & Post-Market Trading Routine

---

# 🎯 Objective

Build a **professional daily trading routine** that eliminates emotional decisions, improves consistency, and ensures every trade follows your trading plan.

> 💡 **Successful trading starts long before you place your first trade.**

---

# 🌅 Pre-Market Routine

Complete this routine **approximately 1 hour before your trading session**.

The goal is to prepare your mind, analyze the market, and avoid impulsive decisions.

---

# ✅ Step 1 — Check the Economic Calendar

Before opening any chart:

- Check today's **high-impact news events**.
    
- Filter news based on the currency pairs you trade.
    
- Mark important announcements.
    

### 🚫 News Trading Rule

Avoid opening new trades:

- **15 minutes before**
    
- **15 minutes after**
    

a high-impact news release.

> 💡 High volatility can invalidate technical setups and trigger stop losses unexpectedly.

---

# ✅ Step 2 — Review Your Trading Plan

Before looking at the charts, remind yourself of your own rules.

Review:

- Charting process
    
- Entry checklist
    
- Trade management rules
    
- Exit strategy
    
- Risk management
    

> 📌 Never rely on memory. Read your trading plan every day.

---

# ✅ Step 3 — Perform Top-Down Analysis

Follow your complete charting process.

### Mark Higher Timeframe Structure

- Swing High
    
- Swing Low
    
- Break of Structure
    
- Market Bias
    

Determine whether today's bias is:

- 🟢 Bullish
    
- 🔴 Bearish
    

---

### Mark Points of Interest

Identify institutional areas such as:

- Supply Zones
    
- Demand Zones
    
- Order Blocks
    
- Fair Value Gaps
    
- Flip Zones
    
- Liquidity Zones
    

These become your potential reaction areas.

---

### Plan Before Price Arrives

Instead of chasing price:

- Define where you want to trade.
    
- Set price alerts.
    
- Wait patiently.
    

> 💡 Professionals prepare first and execute later.

---

# ✅ Step 4 — Review Existing Trades

If holding overnight positions:

Check:

- Stop Loss
    
- Take Profit
    
- Risk Exposure
    

Only make adjustments **if your trading plan allows it.**

Never adjust trades because of fear or hope.

---

# ✅ Step 5 — Mental Preparation

Spend **10–15 minutes** calming your mind.

Examples:

- Deep breathing
    
- Meditation
    
- Visualization
    
- Quiet reflection
    

Purpose:

- Reduce stress
    
- Improve focus
    
- Transition from analysis mode to execution mode
    

> 🧠 A calm trader makes better decisions than an emotional trader.

---

# ⚙️ Mechanical Trading Plan

---

# 📊 Step 1 — Charting & Market Bias

### Determine the Trend

Identify Market Structure:

📈 Bullish

- Higher Highs
    
- Higher Lows
    

📉 Bearish

- Lower Highs
    
- Lower Lows
    

---

### Define the Swing Range

Locate:

- Swing High
    
- Swing Low
    

based on the latest Break of Structure.

This defines your working range.

---

### Apply Premium & Discount

Use Fibonacci:

```text
Settings:

0

0.5

1
```

Draw:

- Swing Low → Swing High (Bullish)
    
- Swing High → Swing Low (Bearish)
    

---

### Premium Zone

Above 50%

➡️ Look for Shorts

---

### Discount Zone

Below 50%

➡️ Look for Longs

---

# 🎯 Step 2 — Define Points of Interest (POI)

Within Premium or Discount identify:

- Supply Zones
    
- Demand Zones
    
- Order Blocks
    
- Flip Zones
    
- Liquidity Zones
    

### Rule

The closer a zone is to the extreme of the range,

the stronger it usually is.

---

### Patience Rule

Never drop to a lower timeframe until price reaches your POI.

No touch = No trade.

---

# 🎯 Step 3 — Entry Confirmation

Once price reaches your POI,

move to your execution timeframe

(typically **15-Minute or 1-Hour**).

Look for confirmation.

---

## ✅ Market Structure Shift

Wait for internal structure to change.

Example:

Price breaks the last Higher Low

➡️ Bearish Shift

or

Price breaks the last Lower High

➡️ Bullish Shift

---

## ✅ Liquidity Sweep

Wait for price to hunt stop losses first.

Examples:

- Sweep of Swing High
    
- Sweep of Swing Low
    
- Equal Highs
    
- Equal Lows
    

After liquidity is collected,

look for confirmation.

---

# 🚀 Step 4 — Execution Models

## 🔥 Aggressive Entry

Enter immediately after:

- Liquidity Sweep
    
- Market Shift
    

Higher reward,

Higher risk.

---

## 🛡️ Conservative Entry

Wait for price to retrace into:

- Supply Zone
    
- Demand Zone
    
- Order Block
    

created after the Market Shift.

Lower risk,

Higher confirmation.

---

# 🎯 Step 5 — Exit Rules

## Stop Loss

Place beyond the structure that invalidates your trade.

Examples:

- Above Supply Zone (Short)
    
- Below Demand Zone (Long)
    

---

## Take Profit

Aim for logical targets.

Examples:

- Opposing Supply/Demand Zone
    
- Liquidity Pool
    
- Swing High
    
- Swing Low
    

Avoid unrealistic expectations.

Take profits where price is likely to react.

---

## Trade Management

This strategy follows a

> **Set & Forget** approach.

After entering:

- No moving Stop Loss
    
- No partial profits
    
- Allow the original plan to play out
    

---

# 🌙 Post-Market Routine

Once your trading session ends,

the learning begins.

---

# 📒 Trading Journal

Record every trade.

Include:

- Entry Screenshot
    
- Exit Screenshot
    
- Trade Idea
    
- Emotions
    
- Mistakes
    
- Lessons Learned
    

Also answer:

- Did I follow my trading plan?
    
- Was my execution disciplined?
    

---

# 📊 Performance Review

Track important statistics.

Examples:

- Win Rate
    
- Average R-Multiple
    
- Profit Factor
    
- Compliance Score
    
- Edge Score
    

Your journal should reveal whether your **process** is improving.

---

# 🚧 Guardrail Review

Identify rule violations.

Examples:

- Trading outside your session
    
- Ignoring Higher Timeframe Bias
    
- Revenge Trading
    
- Overtrading
    
- Skipping confirmation
    

The goal is to eliminate these over time.

---

# 🔄 Reflection

Review your:

- Weekly performance
    
- Monthly performance
    
- Quarterly performance
    
- Yearly performance
    

Ask yourself:

- What worked?
    
- What didn't?
    
- What should I improve?
    

Treat trading like a business audit.

---

# 🧘 Mental Reset

Before ending the day:

- Deep breathing
    
- Meditation
    
- Walk away from charts
    

Do not carry today's emotions into tomorrow's trading session.

Every trading day is a fresh start.

---

# 💡 Key Principles

## ⏳ Waiting Is a Position

Sometimes the most profitable trade is **no trade**.

Patience protects capital.

---

## ⚙️ Focus on Process

Don't try to predict the market.

Focus on executing your system perfectly.

Good execution produces good results over time.

---

## 📋 Clarity Creates Discipline

Following rules is much easier when every step is clearly defined.

A confusing system creates emotional decisions.

A clear system creates confidence.

---

## 🤖 Mechanical Beats Emotional

A mechanical trader relies on:

- Data
    
- Rules
    
- Statistics
    
- Repetition
    

Not on:

- Gut feelings
    
- Hope
    
- Fear
    
- Excitement
    

---

# ⭐ Daily Trading Checklist

## 🌅 Before the Market

-  Check Economic Calendar
    
-  Review Trading Plan
    
-  Mark Higher Timeframe Structure
    
-  Define Daily Bias
    
-  Mark Premium & Discount
    
-  Identify POIs
    
-  Set Alerts
    
-  Review Existing Trades
    
-  Complete Mental Preparation
    

---

## 🎯 Before Every Trade

-  Higher Timeframe Bias confirmed
    
-  Price reached POI
    
-  Liquidity swept
    
-  Market Structure Shift confirmed
    
-  Entry model valid
    
-  Risk-to-Reward acceptable
    
-  Stop Loss defined
    
-  Take Profit defined
    

---

## 🌙 After the Market

-  Journal every trade
    
-  Record emotions
    
-  Calculate performance metrics
    
-  Identify mistakes
    
-  Review rule violations
    
-  Perform mental reset
    

---

# 🏆 Final Takeaways

- Preparation creates confidence.
    
- Trade only when your complete checklist is satisfied.
    
- Let the market come to your zones—never chase it.
    
- Your edge comes from **consistent execution**, not prediction.
    
- Success is built through discipline, journaling, and continuous improvement.
    

> **Golden Rule:**  
> **Professionals don't wake up looking for trades—they wake up looking for reasons _not_ to trade until their rules are fully satisfied.**
